Overview

An overview of my interest in this question - should Monroe County adopt a vote center model over the existing precinct-based voting model? The study (cited in BSquare bulletin) suggested that there was no realistic increase in voter participation (turnout) but it might be more convenient for us, and “everyone else is already doing it”. Although that would also come at a cost, a projected $600,000 or about 5% of the annual budget for this sort of thing.

TL;DR: - Counties that adopted Vote Center generally have lower in-person turnout and higher absentee (including early voting) turnout. By 2020 half (46) of Indiana’s 92 counties had adopted vote centers.
- Among those that adopted Vote Center prior to the 2016 election (about 1/3), a slight improvement in presidential turnout was evident (~5%), but small and mixed effects were present for mid-term elections (’18, ’22).
- Strong, positive effects were seen in vote center adoption for absentee voting (early in-person and by-mail) and similar negative effects for in-person Election day voting, essentially undercutting any argument that Vote Center make in-person voting on election day easier - just the opposite! On average counties that adopted Vote Center saw less in-person voting, even witholding 2020 (health concerns)
- Monroe county has lower-than-average turnout (about 55% 2012 and 2016, about 63% 2020 and 2024) and a high rate of absentee (38->60%) voting that increased and remains high since the pandemic.
- Monroe county also saw a steep drop in registered voters, from a high of 112,228 in 2016 to 100,2528 in 2020 and 93,146 in 2024, a 17% decrease, possibly driven by policies (sending students home mid-March and keeping them online through the fall IIRC) instituted during the pandemic; however the number of voters voting remains around a steady 60,000 during this same period.

It seems to me like adopting a vote center model would be a waste of time and public funds, and that in-person early voting should be expanded instead. In-person Election day voting is lower for all counties that adopted vote center, which is just the opposite effect that it was argued that this would have!

Data Quality

The data generally appear to be accurate, but there may have been some transcription errors resulting in bad counts, as well as other errors (e.g. Decatur below) with no obvious explanation.

Trends in Indiana Voting

Regardless of county, how often do people registered to vote vote in Indiana? It clearly varies by whether it’s a presidential election year or not. 2018 Seems to be an odd sort of year for a number of counties, where before some up-and-down variation was seen, now it’s flat or hardly changed. Maybe worth investigating.

Presidential election years tend to have much higher reported turnout rates, as one might expect given so much of political coverage from the mainstream and new media tend to focus on presidential elections rather than anything else. 2018 appears to stand apart from 2014 and 2022, especially for one county, in that it was a highly movitated year - much higher than expected, and nearly equal to 2016 (a presidential election year). The trend line shows there was a bump in 2018 and slight increase since, but typically hovers around 50% turnout rate from 2010 to 2016 and about 55% after 2018.

Overall, voter turnout in Indiana ranges from X in presidential election years among registered voters (note about data) to Y in off-years.

Vote Center Adoption

The application process (?) to shift from precinct-based to vote-center based elections changed in 2011 or 2012 and made it easier for counties to adopt Vote Center. There was a slow but consistent growth from 2012 to about 2020, after which a the largest number (11) adopted the vote center model, possibly drawing upon the pandemic as a reason - tough to say because I think the process might be multi-year by design. I.e., some of the efforts may have initiated in 2017 but were potentially delayed during the pandemic.

# tidy makes level 1 (Precinct based) estimate1, level2 (Vote center) estimate2; estimate is diff(precinct - vote center)
voting_stats <- left_join(reg_turnout, vote_centers, by = "county") |> 
  mutate(adoption_status = if_else(election_year >= replace_na(adoption_year, 2025),
                                   "Vote Center", "Precinct-based") |> 
           factor()) |> 
  filter(!(county == "Decatur" & election_year == 2022)) |> 
  select(election_year, adoption_year, adoption_status, contains("rate")) |> 
  pivot_longer(contains("rate")) |> 
  rename(x = value, y = adoption_status) |>
  nest(data = c(x, y), .by = c(election_year, name)) |> 
  mutate(t_test = map(data, 
                      \(x) broom::tidy(t.test(x ~ y, 
                                              data = x, 
                                              var.equal = FALSE,
                                              # default is 2-sided but specific argument here is that Vote Center will be higher;
                                              # but since estimate1 is precinct, we're testing that it's lower; else we could just refactor
                                              alternative = "less"))),
         n_vote_centers = map(data, filter, y == "Vote Center") |> 
           map_int(nrow),

Counties that had adopted vote centers have substantially different absentee voting rates - i.e. lower rates of voting in-person on Election Day - but no real differences in general turnout (voting overall), regardless of election type (mid-term or presidential). The elections saw the greatest difference between counties with vote centers and those with traditional precinct-based voting: a -23 percentage-point difference in absentee voting, with 50 of vote center counties voters casting their ballot absentee, compared to just 27 of voters in precinct-based counties.

Code 
x <- left_join(reg_turnout, vote_centers, by = "county") |>

                                   "Vote Center", "Precincts"),

  summarize(

  arrange(county, pres_election_year, adoption_status) |> 
  mutate(

                                     )),
         county = forcats::fct_rev(factor(county)),
         change = if_else(value < 0, "neg", "pos")
  ) |> 
  filter(!is.na(value))
